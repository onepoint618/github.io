Data Driven Decisions

The Cloud Services sales team, solution delivery and operational support teams used their own method to track deals, as they progressed to implementation. Data was manually maintained and often incomplete, causing downstream challenges. I designed and built a Deal Lifecycle Tracking prototype (Minimum Viable Product, MVP) to replace 7 Excel spreadsheets. I mapped data from various sources into a single Power BI dashboard, offering stakeholders a consistent data view and basic reporting. This saved time and enabling better business decision making.

Toolset used was Salesforce, Power BI, Power Query ETL, Power Automate, Excel and SharePoint.

Role: Senior Business Analyst
Industry: IT Services Provider

Process Library

I joined a PMO (Project Management Office) Team to help support the migration of a customers applications to cloud infrastructure. Document governance and management of project operating procedures, reference material and support team processes had become problematic. For example, process descriptions and work instructions couldn't be found, wasting time & effort.

I established a SharePoint based documentation library, including categorisation scheme, search facility and request for help process. I defined and negotiated Librarian, Artefact Owner and Library Owner roles with the PMO and gained stakeholder agreement on the approach. After training and launching, the library became the definitive source for the program team.

Role: Senior Business Analyst
Industry: Public Sector (Europe)

In Search of Blue

As a keen beachcomber, I find solace and a deep connection with nature along the shoreline. I enjoy collecting sea glass, scouring the sands for those elusive gems that have been shaped by the sea.
I've got quite a collection now, but finding true blue sea glass remains a thrilling challenge.
Exploring another approach, to make my own blue sea glass, I built my own wind-powered rock tumbler. Made completely from re-purposed components (junk?), it is functional, although, there's still some fine-tuning needed. The gearing's a bit too enthusiastic, causing it to spin like a centrifuge rather than gently tumble the glass.
The quest continues, and each shoreline visit brings the possibility of discovering a hidden treasure trove of secrets from the sea.
And I have yet to stumble upon a message-in-a-bottle.

Seeds of growth

While I can't exactly claim to be a naturally talented gardener, there's something magical about finding seeds and seeing them sprout from the soil and gradually unfurling their delicate leaves as they grow.
It's a simple yet remarkably satisfying to nurture these little green beings, seeing them thrive when given a little bit of care.
In today's tech-driven world, it's all too easy to lose touch with the simple pleasures of nature. The tangible, tactile act of caring for plants is a grounding experience that helps me find balance in the digital age.

HomeLab RPi

My current home lab is centred around Raspberry Pi experimentation and this is where I feed my inner tech enthusiast.

Currently, it includes a NAS server that takes care of shared storage and backups, housed within a casing I built from a repurposed Cable-TV box generously donated by a friend.
I use PiHole to filtering out those pesky ads from the online experience. Home security is covered with a DIY CCTV system that monitors what's going on outside. And a media centre, that's a work in progress, promising to elevate my entertainment setup to new heights.

For me, the fun is in sucessfully hacking something together to meet my needs. It's not just about the technology; it's about learning and using creativity to build useful things that work.

The World of Web Design

Driven by a previously unsatisfied curiosity about user interface design, particularly websites, I decided to gain some hands-on experience by creating my own portfolio website [You are here!]. I also created a launchpad for my freelance consulting side-hustle website located [here].
My exploration took me through various approaches, from experimenting Static Site Generators (SSGs) like Hugo and Jekyll with templates, to trying out platforms like Wix and Canva. However, I encountered limitations along the way, so after flirting with Bootstrap, I eventually settled on adapting a raw HTML/CSS/JS template to get more control and flexibility.

This journey has been a great learning experience, and now, whenever I encounter a particularly impressive (or not-so-great) website, I can't resist checking out the creator behind it.
